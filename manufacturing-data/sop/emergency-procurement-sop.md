## 1. Purpose & Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This SOP establishes standardized procedures for emergency procurement of critical spare parts, materials, and services required to maintain production continuity and prevent extended operational disruptions.

### 1.2 Scope
Applies to all emergency procurement requests where:
- Standard procurement lead times would result in significant production loss
- Equipment failure creates immediate safety or operational risk
- Single point of failure equipment requires urgent repair
- Customer commitments are at risk due to material/part unavailability

### 1.3 Exclusions
- Routine maintenance parts with adequate inventory
- Planned capital equipment purchases
- Non-critical consumables with alternative solutions available

---

## 2. Definitions

**Emergency Procurement:** Expedited acquisition of goods or services outside normal procurement timelines due to urgent operational need.

**Single Point of Failure (SPOF):** Equipment or process with no backup capacity where failure stops production.

**Critical Spare:** Part or component whose unavailability would cause production stoppage or significant safety risk.

**Expedited Delivery:** Shipping method faster than standard lead time, typically air freight or courier service.

**ROI (Return on Investment):** Financial benefit of emergency procurement compared to cost of production downtime.

## 3. Authorization Levels & Approval Matrix

### 3.1 Procurement Value Thresholds

| Value Range | Approval Authority | Response Time | Documentation Required |
|-------------|-------------------|---------------|------------------------|
| < $5,000 | Maintenance Supervisor | Immediate | Verbal + Email confirmation |
| $5,000 - $25,000 | Maintenance Manager | 2 hours | Emergency Procurement Form |
| $25,000 - $100,000 | Plant Manager | 4 hours | Full EPR + Business Case |
| $100,000 - $500,000 | Operations Director | 8 hours | Full EPR + Executive Summary |
| > $500,000 | VP Operations + CFO | 24 hours | Board-level approval required |

### 3.2 Approval Chain Requirements

**Tier 1: Under $5,000**
- Single approver: Maintenance Supervisor
- Verbal authorization acceptable
- Email confirmation within 24 hours
- Suitable for: Standard spare parts, consumables, minor repairs

**Tier 2: $5,000 - $25,000**
- Primary: Maintenance Manager
- Secondary: Procurement Manager (notification)
- Written authorization required
- Suitable for: Critical spare parts, specialized components

**Tier 3: $25,000 - $100,000**
- Primary: Plant Manager
- Secondary: Maintenance Manager + Procurement Manager
- Full business justification required
- Suitable for: Major assemblies, SPOF equipment parts

**Tier 4: $100,000 - $500,000**
- Primary: Operations Director
- Secondary: Plant Manager + Finance Manager
- Executive summary and ROI analysis required
- Suitable for: Complete system replacements, major overhauls

**Tier 5: Over $500,000**
- Primary: VP Operations + CFO
- Board notification required
- Comprehensive business case with alternatives analysis
- Suitable for: Critical equipment replacement, major capital items

## 4. Decision Matrix

### 4.1 ROI Impact vs. Time Sensitivity Matrix

```
                    TIME SENSITIVITY
                    
R   HIGH    │ TIER 1          │ TIER 2          │ TIER 3          │
O           │ Immediate       │ Same Day        │ 24-48 Hours     │
I           │ Approval        │ Approval        │ Approval        │
            │ Auto-approve    │ Manager level   │ Director level  │
I   ────────┼─────────────────┼─────────────────┼─────────────────┤
M           │                 │                 │                 │
P   MEDIUM  │ TIER 2          │ TIER 3          │ TIER 4          │
A           │ Same Day        │ 24-48 Hours     │ 3-5 Days        │
C           │ Approval        │ Approval        │ Approval        │
T           │ Manager level   │ Plant Manager   │ Operations Dir  │
    ────────┼─────────────────┼─────────────────┼─────────────────┤
            │                 │                 │                 │
    LOW     │ TIER 3          │ TIER 4          │ TIER 5          │
            │ 24-48 Hours     │ 3-5 Days        │ Standard        │
            │ Approval        │ Approval        │ Process         │
            │ Plant Manager   │ Operations Dir  │ Normal channels │
            └─────────────────┴─────────────────┴─────────────────┘
                IMMEDIATE      URGENT (< 48h)    STANDARD (> 48h)
```

### 4.2 ROI Impact Calculation

**HIGH ROI Impact (> 20:1)**
- Production loss > $100K per day
- Customer penalties > $500K
- Safety-critical equipment
- Single point of failure with no workaround

**MEDIUM ROI Impact (5:1 to 20:1)**
- Production loss $25K - $100K per day
- Customer impact moderate
- Partial production capability available
- Workarounds exist but inefficient

**LOW ROI Impact (< 5:1)**
- Production loss < $25K per day
- Minimal customer impact
- Alternative solutions available
- Can defer to standard procurement

### 4.3 Time Sensitivity Classification

**IMMEDIATE (< 4 hours)**
- Production currently stopped
- Safety hazard present
- Customer shipment due within 24 hours
- No inventory buffer available

**URGENT (4-48 hours)**
- Production will stop within 48 hours
- Critical customer commitment at risk
- Inventory buffer < 2 days
- Preventive action to avoid crisis

**STANDARD (> 48 hours)**
- Production continues with reduced capacity
- Adequate time for supplier coordination
- Inventory buffer > 2 days
- Planned maintenance window available

## 5. Emergency Procurement Request (EPR) Process

### 5.1 Request Initiation
1. Maintenance/Operations identifies critical need
2. Complete Emergency Procurement Request form
3. Calculate ROI and time sensitivity
4. Determine approval tier from matrix
5. Submit to appropriate authority

### 5.2 Required Documentation

**Minimum Requirements (All Tiers):**
- Part number and description
- Quantity required
- Estimated cost
- Supplier information
- Delivery timeline
- Business justification

**Additional for Tier 3+ ($25K+):**
- Production impact analysis
- Customer impact assessment
- ROI calculation
- Alternative solutions considered
- Risk assessment if not approved

**Additional for Tier 4+ ($100K+):**
- Executive summary
- Financial impact model
- Supplier comparison (minimum 2 quotes)
- Long-term prevention plan

### 5.3 Approval Workflow

```
[Request Initiated]
        ↓
[Determine Value Tier]
        ↓
[Route to Approver(s)]
        ↓
[Review & Decision] ←─── [Request More Info]
        ↓                        ↑
[Approved?] ─── NO ──────────────┘
        ↓ YES
[Issue PO]
        ↓
[Expedite Delivery]
        ↓
[Track & Receive]
        ↓
[Post-Procurement Review]
```

## 6. Supplier Management

### 6.1 Pre-Qualified Emergency Suppliers
Maintain list of strategic suppliers with:
- 24/7 contact information
- Expedited delivery capabilities
- Quality certifications
- Emergency pricing agreements
- Stock availability commitments

### 6.2 Supplier Selection Criteria
1. **Availability:** Can deliver within required timeline
2. **Quality:** Meets or exceeds OEM specifications
3. **Price:** Reasonable premium for expedited service
4. **Reliability:** Proven track record
5. **Proximity:** Geographic advantage for faster delivery

### 6.3 Emergency Delivery Options (Priority Order)
1. **Same-Day Courier:** 2-6 hours, $500-$2,000 premium
2. **Next-Day Air:** 24 hours, $200-$800 premium
3. **2-Day Air:** 48 hours, $100-$400 premium
4. **Ground Expedited:** 3-5 days, $50-$200 premium
5. **Stock Transfer:** Internal or partner facility, minimal cost

## 7. Cost Management

### 7.1 Acceptable Premium Guidelines

| Standard Cost | Acceptable Emergency Premium | Maximum Total Cost |
|---------------|------------------------------|-------------------|
| < $1,000 | Up to 200% | $3,000 |
| $1,000 - $10,000 | Up to 150% | $25,000 |
| $10,000 - $50,000 | Up to 100% | $100,000 |
| $50,000 - $100,000 | Up to 75% | $175,000 |
| > $100,000 | Up to 50% | Case-by-case |

**Justification Required When:**
- Premium exceeds guidelines above
- Total cost exceeds approval tier threshold
- Alternative suppliers offer significantly lower cost
- Downtime cost doesn't justify premium

### 7.2 Cost Components to Consider
- Base part/material cost
- Expedited shipping charges
- Supplier rush fees
- Customs/import expediting (if international)
- Installation labor (overtime if applicable)
- Quality inspection costs

## 8. Risk Assessment & Mitigation

### 8.1 Pre-Procurement Risk Checklist
- [ ] Part compatibility verified by engineering
- [ ] Supplier quality certifications reviewed
- [ ] Delivery timeline confirmed and realistic
- [ ] Installation resources available
- [ ] Alternative solutions evaluated
- [ ] Customer communication plan in place
- [ ] Financial impact calculated
- [ ] Approval authority confirmed

### 8.2 Mitigation Strategies

**If Part Unavailable:**
- Contact alternative suppliers immediately
- Explore refurbished/rebuilt options
- Consider temporary repair solutions
- Evaluate borrowing from partner facilities
- Assess production rescheduling options

**If Delivery Delayed:**
- Escalate with supplier management
- Arrange company pickup if possible
- Implement temporary workarounds
- Communicate revised timeline to stakeholders
- Update production schedule

**If Cost Exceeds Budget:**
- Seek higher approval authority
- Negotiate with supplier
- Evaluate partial quantity procurement
- Consider alternative specifications
- Assess lease/rental options

## 9. Post-Procurement Actions

### 9.1 Immediate Follow-Up
- Quality inspection upon receipt
- Installation coordination
- Performance verification
- Documentation filing
- Inventory system update

### 9.2 Root Cause Analysis (Required for Tier 3+)
Within 30 days of emergency procurement:
- Identify why emergency was necessary
- Evaluate inventory management practices
- Assess preventive maintenance effectiveness
- Review supplier lead time accuracy
- Recommend process improvements

### 9.3 Continuous Improvement
- Update critical spare parts list
- Adjust inventory reorder points
- Negotiate better supplier agreements
- Enhance predictive maintenance
- Document lessons learned

## 13. Industry Best Practices & Benchmarks

### 13.1 Threshold Recommendations (Based on Industry Standards)

The $5,000 threshold for Tier 1 approval is based on:
- **Manufacturing Industry Average:** Most facilities set supervisor-level authority at $3K-$7K
- **Risk-Benefit Analysis:** Below $5K, approval delays often cost more than procurement risk
- **Audit Efficiency:** Reduces administrative burden while maintaining control
- **Empowerment Balance:** Enables rapid response without excessive authority

### 13.2 Industry Benchmarks

**Emergency Procurement Frequency:**
- World-class: < 3 per quarter
- Industry average: 5-8 per quarter
- Needs improvement: > 10 per quarter

**Approval Response Times:**
- Best-in-class: 80% approved within target time
- Industry average: 60% within target time
- Below standard: < 50% within target time

**Cost Premium Acceptance:**
- Typical range: 50-150% for critical parts
- Acceptable for SPOF: Up to 200%
- Red flag: > 200% without exceptional justification

### 13.3 Recommended Practices from Industry Leaders

1. **Predictive Maintenance Integration:** Use IoT sensors to predict failures before emergency procurement needed
2. **Strategic Inventory:** Maintain 2-week buffer stock for all SPOF equipment parts
3. **Supplier Partnerships:** Negotiate standing emergency agreements with 3-5 key suppliers
4. **Cross-Industry Collaboration:** Establish part-sharing agreements with non-competing manufacturers
5. **Digital Approval Systems:** Implement mobile approval apps for faster authorization
6. **Quarterly Reviews:** Analyze emergency procurement patterns to improve preventive maintenance
